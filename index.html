Featured project

ROS 2 OpenArm Pick-and-Place

A ROS 2 (Jazzy) simulation of a 7-DOF OpenArm manipulator that picks parcels off a table and inserts them into a box, then extends to two arms with independent and cooperative (bimanual) modes. Built as a personal reimplementation on the open-source OpenArm.

Simulation only. Grasping uses a kinematic “attach to gripper frame” step (not contact physics); a MuJoCo physics phase with real grasping was planned but not reached.

  • FK/IK on a 7-DOF redundant arm (roboticstoolbox-python, Levenberg–Marquardt) validated to 0.0 mm position error, with tolerance-based acceptance to work around the solver’s false-negative flag.
  • Resolved-rate Cartesian control (body-frame Jacobian, damped least-squares near singularities) for straight-line, constant-orientation box insertion.
  • Dual-arm via a programmatically composed two-arm URDF, plus two coordination modes: a mutex-token shared workspace (collision-free by construction) and bimanual cooperative carry with a mid-motion grip-posture switch.
  • Modular node pipeline: target publisher → IK planner → joint-state bridge → robot_state_publisher → TF → rviz2.

About

A robotics engineer with a neuro background

I’m a robotics and control engineer based in Reading, UK, finishing a PhD in biomedical engineering. I lead with human-centred and medical robotics, where my EMG/EEG and embodiment research is a genuine differentiator, while staying hands-on with general robot-arm manipulation: ROS 2, kinematics, and motion planning.
